Data Guide

Click each section below to see where to find the data the tool needs. Open example_config.yaml next to this guide and fill in your numbers as you go.

Minimum viable run (5 minutes)

If you just want a quick estimate, you only need two numbers:

  • electricity.annual_kwh — your annual electricity use (kWh). Find this on your latest bill.
  • gas.annual_kwh — your annual gas use (kWh). Same bill, or omit this section if you have no gas.

Everything else (HDF data, EV details, current plan id) is optional. Without them the tool uses a default Irish residential load profile and treats your current plan as the Electric Ireland Home Electric+ Saver 28% baseline.

Electricity

electricity.annual_kwh — Your yearly electricity usage in kWh

Where to find it

  1. Easiest: Look at your latest electricity bill. Most Irish bills include an "Annual Estimated Consumption" or 12-month total near the unit-rate breakdown.
  2. Most accurate: Add up the kWh from every bill in the last 12 months (usually 6 bills, every 2 months).
  3. From smart meter: Log in to your supplier's online portal — they show a 12-month chart you can read off.
  4. From ESB Networks HDF: Sum the "Active Import Interval (kWh)" column in your half-hour CSV — most accurate, but takes longer to set up. See the next section.

Sanity range

Typical Irish 2–3 person household: 3,500–4,500 kWh/year. Family of 4–5: 5,000–6,500 kWh. With an EV charging at home: add 1,500–3,000 kWh.

electricity.meter_type — Smart, standard, or day/night meter?

How to tell

Check your most recent bill or the meter itself:

  • smart — Bill mentions "Smart Tariff" / "MCC12" / "MCC16" / "Time of Use". The meter has a digital display that shows several rate periods (Day, Night, Peak).
  • day_night — Bill has separate "Day rate" and "Night rate" lines but no Peak. Older Nightsaver meters. The physical meter has two digital counters.
  • standard_24hr — A single unit rate on the bill. Most older meters.

If unsure, check ESB Networks' "My Account" page — your meter category code is shown there. v0 supports smart fully and treats the other two as smart equivalents (catalogue expansion to dedicated non-smart plans is on the roadmap).

electricity.hdf_csv_path — (optional) ESB Networks half-hour data

What this is

Your smart meter sends a kWh reading every 30 minutes. ESB Networks gives you a 2-year CSV export of that data. When the simulator has this, it uses your actual hourly consumption pattern instead of a default profile — much more accurate for time-of-use plans (especially with EV charging).

How to download

  1. Sign up / log in at myaccount.esbnetworks.ie (separate from your supplier's account).
  2. You'll need your MPRN (see next section) and a recent ESB Networks bill to register.
  3. Menu → My Smart Data Tools → Download My Data.
  4. Choose HDF format and the date range (the tool can use as little as 30 days, but 6–12 months gives a much more reliable seasonal pattern).
  5. Email arrives within minutes; download the CSV.
Latency: ESB Networks publishes data with a 2–3 day delay. If you just installed your smart meter, wait at least 2–3 days before trying to download.

What the file looks like

Filename will be something like HDF_calckWh_10001234567_31-05-2026.csv (the 11-digit number is your MPRN; the date is the export end date).

Set hdf_csv_path: /absolute/path/to/HDF_calckWh_*.csv in your config. The simulator will report how many days it found.

electricity.ev_start_date — When you started charging an EV at home

Only relevant if you have an EV and your HDF spans a period both before and after you started home-charging. The simulator uses only pre-EV readings to extract your baseload (so EV charging doesn't double-count when the simulator adds the EV layer back on top).

  • Got your EV after the HDF period started → set ev_start_date to the date you first plugged in at home.
  • Already had EV when HDF starts → omit or set to null; whole HDF will be used as "with-EV" pattern.
  • No EV → ignore.
electricity.ev — EV usage details

annual_kwh

How much you charge at home per year (kWh). Find it in:

  • Tesla: Tesla app → Charging Stats (energy added at home only).
  • myenergi Zappi: myenergi app → Stats → totals tab.
  • Easee: Easee app → home unit → energy used.
  • Other smart charger: Whatever total energy reading the charger app provides.
  • Rough estimate: Annual km × kWh/100km ÷ home-charging fraction. Example: a Tesla Model Y driver doing 15,000 km/year at ~17 kWh/100 km, charging 85% at home, charges about 3,000 kWh/year at home. Sample household in the verification dataset comes out around 2,022 kWh because they do more public charging.

can_schedule_charging

Can your charger be set to a fixed daily window (e.g. 02:00–05:00)?

  • true — myenergi Zappi (Boost Timer), Tesla (Scheduled Charging), Easee (Smart Charging), most modern EV chargers. The simulator runs a "Shifted" scenario that puts all EV kWh in each plan's cheapest band.
  • false — Older chargers or always charging on arrival. Only "Now" cost is computed.
electricity.current_plan.plan_id — Which plan you're on now

Used to build the BASELINE row in your result (so you can see how much switching saves). The id must match an entry in tariffs/electricity.yaml.

How to find your current plan id

  1. Look at your most recent bill — the plan name is usually at the top (e.g. "Home Electric+ Saver", "Smart Standard Dual Fuel", "EV Smart").
  2. Open tariffs/electricity.yaml and search for that name. The id is the id: field on the matching entry.
  3. If your plan isn't in the catalogue, either: (a) pick the closest match, or (b) add a custom plan in the config — see Example 3 in example_config.yaml.

Example ids: ei_home_electric_saver_28pc, bg_smart_standard_dual_fuel_2026q2, energia_smart_data_mcc12_2026q2, flogas_ev_night_charge_2026q2.

electricity.current_plan.discount_expires_on — Your discount end date

When the introductory discount on your current plan runs out. Used to model the "DO-NOTHING" row in the result (what you'd pay if you let your discount lapse and rolled onto the standard rate).

  • Look on your supplier's welcome email, latest bill, or My Account page.
  • It is exactly 12 months from your sign-up date for most fixed-discount Irish plans.
  • Omit if you've been with the same supplier on standard rates for a long time (DO-NOTHING row will not be shown).

Gas

Omit the gas: section entirely if you have no mains gas

The simulator handles electricity-only households correctly. If you heat with a heat pump or oil, just remove the whole gas: block from my_config.yaml.

gas.annual_kwh — Yearly gas usage in kWh

From your latest gas bill or by summing 12 months of bills. Typical Irish 2-bed apartment with gas hob only: 1,500–3,000 kWh/year. Average 3-bed semi with gas central heating: 11,000 kWh/year. Bigger or poorly insulated house: 15,000–22,000 kWh/year.

Important: Irish gas bills sometimes report in m³ (cubic meters) instead of kWh. To convert: multiply m³ by ~11.0 (the conversion factor is shown on the bill, usually around 10.8–11.3 depending on calorific value).

gas.current_plan.plan_id — Your current gas plan

Same approach as electricity. Look at your bill, find the plan name, look it up in tariffs/gas.yaml. If you're on a dual-fuel bundle, the gas plan id usually has the bundle discount in the name (e.g. ei_gas_dual_plus_8_5pc, energia_gas_27pc_with_smart_data).

Tariff catalogue — fixing wrong rates

The shipped catalogue says X but my quote says Y

Rates change a few times per year. If the rate on your personal quote disagrees with our YAML, you have three escape hatches — none of them requires editing the shipped YAML.

Override a single field

tariff_overrides:
  flogas_ev_night_charge_2026q2:
    standing_eur_per_year: 320.00
    welcome_credit_eur: 50

Override a banded rate table wholesale

tariff_overrides:
  energia_smart_data_mcc12_2026q2:
    rates_inc_vat:
      kind: bands
      bands:
        - {hours: [17, 19], rate_cpkwh: 34.10, label: Peak}
        - {hours: [23, 24], rate_cpkwh: 16.50, label: Night}
        - {hours: [0, 8],   rate_cpkwh: 16.50, label: Night}
        - {hours: [8, 17],  rate_cpkwh: 30.20, label: Day}
        - {hours: [19, 23], rate_cpkwh: 30.20, label: Day}

Add a brand-new plan + rank it

custom_electricity_plans:
  - id: my_personal_energia_quote_2026_06
    supplier: Energia
    label: "My Energia quote (10 May 2026)"
    # ... full plan schema, see Example 3 in example_config.yaml

custom_combos:
  - electricity_id: my_personal_energia_quote_2026_06
    gas_id: energia_gas_27pc_with_smart_data
    label: "My personal Energia quote + Energia Gas 27%"

Overridden / custom plans are tagged [USER OVERRIDE] in the output so it's obvious which numbers are yours, not ours.

Where to find supporting reference data

MPRN (Meter Point Reference Number)

11-digit number printed on every electricity bill and on the front of your meter. Needed to register on ESB Networks (for HDF download). Format: 10001234567 — starts with 1 in the Republic of Ireland.

GPRN (Gas Point Reference Number)

7-digit number on every gas bill. Format: 1234567. Needed when you switch suppliers but the tool doesn't ask for it directly.

Eircode

On every utility bill. The 3-character prefix (e.g. D09, K67) is what the config asks for — used to flag regional plan eligibility in the future. Not load-bearing in v0.

Smart meter type codes (MCC)
CodeWhat it means
MCC01Standard 24-hour meter (non-smart)
MCC02Day/Night meter (non-smart, 2-band)
MCC12Smart, 30-minute interval, NOT publishing to ESB Networks data feed
MCC16Smart, 30-minute interval, publishing to data feed (you can download HDF)

Some Energia plans are MCC-specific (their Smart Data plan is MCC12-only; their Smart Standard is MCC16-only). If you don't know your MCC, check your meter — Irish smart meters have a sticker, or call ESB Networks customer service.

Once you've filled in my_config.yaml

conda activate energy-moneysaver
python -m src.cli --config my_config.yaml

This prints a ranked comparison to the terminal. For the full report — cost breakdown, methodology, and source links — use the web app.

If the simulator complains about a missing plan_id, double-check it against tariffs/electricity.yaml / tariffs/gas.yaml.
